A monthly update of harassment and displacement risk in rent stabilized buildings in Queens Community Board 10. Risk indicators include sales, HPD complaints, HPD violations, DOB complaints, and DOB construction permit applications. Buildings that appear on multiple lists are coded yellow, orange, or red, based on the number of lists they're on.

The blue links let you click through directly to a property's Who Owns What page for owner info, HPD building info, DOB's Building Information System, ACRIS, or property tax information. You can also click a building address to view it in Google Maps.

All buildings listed have registered stabilized units at least once between 2007 and 2016 (Notes).

If a building appears on more than one of the lists below in a given month it will be flagged:

New sales of residential buildings last month

A large sale price can mean the new owner plans to displace tenants to make more profit. Buildings on this list sold for more than $50,000, which we use as a proxy for "real" sales versus other types of transfers. Buildings with 10+ new HPD violations last month

HPD issues violations of the Housing Maintenance Code after inspecting building conditions. Class A, B, and C violations can indicate a landlord is neglecting or actively harassing tenants, Class C being the most severe (info, p.7). Click a building's (HPD) link for more details.
